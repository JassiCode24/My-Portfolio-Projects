Tribute Page Samuel Morse

Samuel F. B. Morse

A portrait painter and inventor of the electric telegraph

  • 1791 Samuel Finley Breese Morse is born on April 27 in Charlestown, Massachusetts.
  • 1805 went on to Yale College to study religious philosophy, mathematics, and science.
  • 1811 Allston arranged a three-year stay for painting study in England with Morse's father. Morse perfected his painting techniques under Allston's watchful eye, he gained admittance to the Royal Academy at the end of the year.
  • 1815 He left England to return to the United States and begin his full-time career as a painter. Morse was elected a member of the American Antiquarian Society in 1815.
  • 1825 Completes a successful portrait of the Marquis de Lafayette. Becomes a founding member of the National Academy of Design.
  • 1830-1832 Travels to Europe to study art and he painted The Gallery of the Louvre. He begins to develop ideas for the telegraph when returning from Europe in 1832.
  • 1837 He was a co-developer of Morse code and helped to develop the commercial use of telegraphy.
  • 1844 On May 24, sends the first long-distance message via telegraph from Washington, D.C., to Baltimore, Maryland, proclaiming “What hath God wrought.”
  • 1847 Receives a patent for the telegraph.
  • 1851 The Morse telegraphic apparatus was officially adopted as the standard for European telegraphy.
  • 1858 Introduced wired communication to Latin America when he established a telegraph system in Puerto Rico, then a Spanish Colony. He was also elected a foreign member of the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences.
  • 1872 Samuel Morse passes away on April 2 in New York City at age 80.

"Science and art are not opposed." ~ Samuel Morse
